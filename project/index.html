The Purpose of This Site

The purpose of this site is to provide valuable information for young Latter-day saints who are preparing to serve a mission in Ghana. This page talks about the missions established, temples closest to those missions a little about the mission presidents, MTCs, food, languages, means of transport for missionaries, climate.

Brief History of The Church in Ghana

The church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints in Ghana began in the 1960s through literature, with official organization starting in 1973 after the priesthood revelation. Joseph William Billy Johnson and others such as Frank Mensah were instrumental in spreading the faith and organizing congregations in the Cape Coast area by 1972.

The initial establishment was officially recognized by Salt-Lake in 1978 following the announcement of the revelation extending the priesthood and temple blessings to all worthy male members, the first formal missionaries Elder Rendell N. Mabey and sister Rachel Mabey, arrived in 1978.

Languages spoken In Ghana

Ghana is a multilingual language with over 80 languages spoken across its regions. While English is the official language used for government, education and business, the widely spoken language is the Akan language [Twi and Fante followed by Ewe, Ghanaian Pidgin English and Ga.

Staple Foods in Ghana

The most common food across all regions in Ghana is Rice. It is usually accompanied with stew, sauce or soup. It is prepared differently depending on the accompaniment. It could be prepared as a breakfast cereal with a lot of water, widely called rice water or rice porridge Foods in Ghana are dominated by corn hence, most meals have high carbohydrate content. Meals like Banku from maize, Akple which is made with cassava, Boiled yam and Gari

Climate In Ghana

Ghana has a tropical climate characterized by year-round warm temperatures, with distinct wet and dry seasons driven by the Intertropical Discontinuity (ITD). The south has two rainy seasons (April-June) and Sept-Nov while the north has one long wet season (Dec-March)
